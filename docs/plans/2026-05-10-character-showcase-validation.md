## Goal

Validate whether Character Showcase can reliably produce ad-feeling material for the current GOBS marketer workflow.

This run uses 5 characters and 2 directions per character:

- Character reveal.
- Skill or selling-point showcase.

## Current Decision

Decision: `continue`

Reason: the validation ledger has 5 usable samples out of 10. This passes the 3/10 exit threshold, but the continue decision is constrained to single-character reveal and skill-payoff directions.

## Exit Rule

If fewer than 3 out of 10 validation samples are usable, Character Showcase must be marked `experimental` or `pause` and must not be presented as stable in the default flow.

## Recommended Template Directions

- Boss/hero reveal with clean face or silhouette payoff.
- Skill flex with one clear VFX beat.
- Reward payoff when the reward object is simple and does not require exact UI readability.

## Not Recommended

- Multi-character group shots.
- Pet or mount interactions where subjects merge.
- UI-heavy reward panels where text/buttons must remain exact.
- Long environment storytelling without a clear character payoff.

## Fit Summary

Character Showcase is better suited for short video material when the source character art is clean and the action direction is single-focus. Some failed cases are still useful as Banner/key-art prompts, but should not be treated as ready video outputs.

## Product Boundary

Character Showcase can continue as an active Advanced Studio path, but the entry and presets should keep the constraint visible: single-character reveal or skill payoff first, avoid multi-character and UI-heavy directions.
